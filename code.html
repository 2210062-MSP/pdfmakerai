Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about building free websites.

GitHub Pages is completely free for public repositories. You only need to pay if you want to keep your source code private (which doesn't affect the website hosting). There are no hosting fees, bandwidth limits, or other hidden costs for static websites.

Free hosting is ideal for static websites (HTML, CSS, JavaScript). Limitations include lack of server-side scripting (PHP, databases), no professional email hosting, and custom domain setup may require additional DNS configuration. For most personal portfolios and small business sites, these limitations don't pose problems.

Yes! Most free hosting platforms allow custom domains. GitHub Pages and Netlify both support custom domains, though you'll need to purchase the domain separately (typically $10-15/year). They provide instructions for configuring DNS settings to point to your free hosted site.
